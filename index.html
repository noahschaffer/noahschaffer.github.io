ABOUT

I am a fourth year BS/MS student at Northwestern University. I am currently a researcher in the Interactive Audio Lab under the supervision of Prof. Bryan Pardo and Ethan Manilow doing work in audio source separation

My research interests lie at the intersection of Machine Learning and Digital Signal Processing, specifically source separation, generative models for audio, and sound event detection.

PROJECTS

MSG: Make it Sound Good

Abstract:
Slides:

MSG (Make it Sound Good) is a post-processing Generative Adversarial Network for source separation. We use this model to enhance the quality of source separation output by removing aritifacts and reconstructing missing frequencies. Work was presented at the MDX Workshop at ISMIR 2021
